About Me

My name is Alex Brogden, I am 19 years old, and I am from Manchester, England. I am currently an apprentice software developer at Bury College.

Around 2016/2017 I started to develop a fascination with music and the culture surrounding it, particularly experimental hip hop.

I discovered many artists in this time period, all of them producing hip hop music. I use the term hip hop very loosely as these artists combine elements from multiple genres of music such as punk and rock, rather than conforming to the traditional styles of rap.

This journey into the world of experimental hip hop led me to discover one artist that stuck out to me from the rest: Playboi Carti. Since 2017 he has grown from an underground artist with a small cult-like following to one of the largest hip hop artists in the world, headlining huge festivals such as Rolling Loud and Wireless.

Self Titled

On the 14th April 2017 Carti released his debut mixtape Playboi Carti. This mixtape brought a new sound to the hip hop scene, putting a much larger emphasis on the production of the songs and nowhere near as much emphasis on the lyrical content. A few other artists around this time were also releasing this style of music and it became dubbed Mumble Rap.

Die Lit

Just over a year later, Carti released his debut album Die Lit. This album expanded on the sound he helped establish in his previous mixtape and even took it further with songs like FlatBed Freestyle having almost unintelligible lyrics. This mixtape also began to include elements from other genres such as the album cover portraying a punk concert.

Whole Lotta Red

After a build-up of over 2 years Carti finally released his album Whole Lotta Red on 25th December 2020. This album once again introduced a completely new sound which can best be described as genre-bending. It debuted at number 1 on the US Billboard 200.
